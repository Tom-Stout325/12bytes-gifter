Help & Getting Started

New to Gifter? Follow these steps to create your account, log in, and set up your profile.

1) Register for an Account

Go to the registration page to create your account.

Register

What you’ll enter
  • First Name
  • Last Name
  • Username (unique)
  • Email
  • Password + Confirm Password
Notes
  • Your profile will be created automatically.
  • Keep your password private and strong.
  • Your account will need to be approved by the admin before you have full acess.
2) Log In

Already registered? Log in here.

Log In Forgot password?
What you’ll enter
  • Username
  • Password
Tips
  • After logging in, you’ll be routed where you need to go automatically.
  • If your profile isn’t approved yet, you can still view your profile page.
3) Complete Your Profile

Next, set up the details that help your family pick great gifts.

Go to Profile Setup

What you’ll enter
  • Role (Parent or Child)
  • Family / Household (optional at first)
  • Birthday and Anniversary (optional)
  • Avatar: Default, pick from Gallery, or Upload a photo
  • Sizes: Shirt, Pants, Shoe
  • Favorites: Hobbies/Sports, Stores, Websites
  • Private Notes (Parents only)
How it works
  • Parents can manage family profiles.
  • Children can’t see private notes—those are visible only to Parents.
  • You can change your avatar anytime (Default, Gallery, or Upload).
  • Click Save to finish. You can always edit later.
After setup, you can visit your profile page. If approval is required, you’ll still see your own profile while it’s pending.

3) Complete Your Profile

Your profile is how your family learns about you at a glance. Filling it out makes it easier for everyone to pick gifts you’ll actually love.

Go to Profile Setup

Profile details you can add
  • Role (Parent or Child) – determines what you can see and manage in the family.
  • Family / Household – connects you to the right group so your wishlists show up for the right people.
  • Birthday & Anniversary – used to highlight important dates so your family sees what’s coming up next.
  • Avatar – pick a default, choose from the gallery, or upload a photo so people can quickly recognize you.
  • Sizes – shirt, pants, and shoe sizes help gift-givers buy clothes that actually fit.
  • Favorites – list your hobbies, sports, favorite stores, and websites to give everyone easy gift ideas.
  • Private Notes (Parents only) – parents can store extra notes about children that kids won’t see, but other parents can use when shopping.
Why this matters
  • Your details drive the reminders and “Coming Up Next” sections for your family.
  • Sizes and favorites keep people from guessing and buying things you won’t use.
  • Parents can manage and update child profiles as things change over time.
  • You can come back to this page anytime to update your info as your interests change.
After you save your profile, you can visit your profile page to review everything. If approval is required, you’ll still be able to see your own profile while it’s pending.
4) Create & Use Wishlists

Once your profile is set up, the next step is to create wishlists. Wishlists tell your family what you’d actually like to receive and help avoid duplicate or unwanted gifts.

How to add wishlist items
  1. Go to your profile page after logging in.
  2. Open your wishlist area or tab.
  3. Use the “Add” button to create a new wishlist item.
  4. Fill in the item name, optional details (size, color, links, notes, etc.).
  5. Save the item. It will appear on your wishlist for your family to see.
What to include in an item
  • Item name – what you want (e.g., “Wireless headphones”).
  • Details – brand, color, size, or anything that matters.
  • Links – optional store links to make it easy to buy.
  • Notes – anything else that will help someone pick the right version.
Who can see what?
  • Your own wishlist – you can see all of your items and their details, but you will not see who has claimed items for you (no spoilers).
  • Other people’s wishlists – family members can view each other’s wishlists to get ideas and claim gifts.
  • Claiming gifts – when someone claims an item, other shoppers with access can see that it’s taken so they don’t buy the same thing.
  • Children & surprises – children won’t see who claimed items on their own wishlist, which keeps the surprise for special occasions.
  • Parents – parents can usually see more detail (including claims) so they can coordinate gifts and avoid duplicates.
Tip: Keep your wishlist updated year-round. Adding ideas as you think of them makes birthdays and holidays easier for everyone.
Need more help? Reach out to your family admin or use the site’s contact option.
